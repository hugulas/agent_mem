# GUI-Rise: Structured Reasoning and History Summarization for GUI

> Academic paper | 2025-10

## 1. Core Claim
- Current GUI navigation agents suffer from poor cross-domain generalization and ineffective history utilization.
- A framework integrating structured reasoning, action prediction, and history summarization—with a history-aware reward directly linking summary quality to action performance—achieves state-of-the-art results, especially in out-of-domain scenarios.

## 2. Method / Mechanism
- **Structured reasoning component**: Generates Chain-of-Thought analyses combining progress estimation and decision reasoning.
- **History summarization**: Produces compact summaries of prior steps to inform future actions.
- **GUI-Rise agent**: Trained via supervised fine-tuning (SFT) on pseudo-labeled trajectories followed by reinforcement learning with GRPO.
- **History-aware reward**: Directly links the quality of generated history summaries to subsequent action performance, ensuring summaries are task-relevant.

## 3. Key Evidence
- **State-of-the-art results** on standard GUI navigation benchmarks under identical training data conditions.
- **Strong out-of-domain performance**: Demonstrates robust generalization across diverse GUI tasks.
- GUI-Rise-3B achieves **36.6%** on OSWorld vs. Qwen2.5-VL-3B at **23.3%**.
- UI-TARS-2B baseline: 6.5% (15 steps); GUI-Rise-2B: 8.7% (15 steps).
- Overall benchmark results show consistent gains across Mind2Web, AITW, and OSWorld.

## 4. Limitations & Gaps
- SFT depends on pseudo-labeled trajectory quality; errors in pseudo-labels propagate to the trained agent.
- History summarization is still lossy compared to full history retention; critical details may be omitted.
- GRPO training is computationally expensive and may not be feasible for rapid iteration or small teams.
- Evaluation focuses on navigation tasks; complex creative or analytical GUI workflows are not tested.

## 5. Relevance to Our Review
- **GUI agents memory**: A leading example of history summarization with performance-linked rewards in GUI agents.
- **Hierarchical memory**: The structured reasoning → action prediction → history summarization pipeline is a form of hierarchical processing.
