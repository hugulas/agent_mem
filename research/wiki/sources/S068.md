## 1. Core Claim
- LLM-powered knowledge bases can replace traditional RAG pipelines by treating a curated markdown wiki as the single source of truth, with the LLM acting as a compiler/query engine rather than a retrieval-augmented generator.

## 2. Method / Mechanism
- **Three-layer structure**: `raw/` (ingested sources), `wiki/` (curated markdown articles), `schema/` (structured metadata and typing).
- **LLM as Compiler**: Raw inputs are processed into wiki articles; the LLM "compiles" knowledge rather than retrieving chunks.
- **Obsidian as IDE**: Uses Obsidian for local markdown editing and navigation.
- **Scale**: ~100 articles, ~400K words in Karpathy's public example.
- **Quality Assurance**: Health checks and linting ensure consistency; outputs are filed back into the wiki (self-healing).

## 3. Key Evidence
- 1.2M+ views on the original post (viral adoption signal).
- ~100 articles / ~400K words demonstrates viability at medium scale.
- Health checks and linting pipeline described as part of the workflow.
