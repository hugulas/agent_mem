> Abstract
> Large Language Model (LLM)-based agents solve complex tasks through iterative
> reasoning, exploration, and tool-use, a process that can result in long, expensive
> context histories. While state-of-the-art Software Engineering (SE) agents like
> OpenHands or Cursor use LLM-based summarization to tackle this issue, it is
> unclear whether the increased complexity offers tangible performance benefits
> compared to simply omitting older observations. We present a systematic com-
> parison of these approaches with SWE-agent on SWE-bench Verified across five
> diverse model configurations. Moreover, we show initial evidence of our findings
> generalizing to the OpenHands agent scaffold. We find that a simple environment
> Observation Masking strategy halves cost relative to the Raw Agent while matching,
> and sometimes slightly exceeding, the solve rate of LLM-Summary. Additionally,
> we introduce a novel hybrid approach that further reduces costs by 7% and 11%
> compared to just Observation Masking or LLM-Summary, respectively. Our find-
> ings raise concerns regarding the trend towards pure LLM-Summary and provide
> initial evidence of untapped cost reductions by pushing the efficiency-effectiveness
> frontier. We release code and data for reproducibility.12

> Introduction
> The ambition to create autonomous agents that can independently handle complex SE tasks is rapidly
> becoming a reality. These agents, powered by LLMs, typically operate in an iterative loop [39, 29],
> at each turn they reason about the current state, devise a plan, and execute a tool (e.g., read a file, run
> tests). The output, or observation, from this tool is then added to the agent’s context for the next turn,
> extending its problem-solving trajectory (Figure 3). This agentic loop acts as a powerful test-time

## 1. Core Claim
- LLM-based summarization for agent context management may be unnecessarily complex; a simple environment Observation Masking strategy (omitting older observations) halves cost while matching or exceeding the solve rate of LLM-Summary.
- A novel hybrid approach further pushes the efficiency-effectiveness frontier, reducing costs by 7% vs. masking and 11% vs. LLM-Summary.

## 2. Method / Mechanism
- **Observation Masking**: Simply omits older environment observations from the context window while preserving recent ones and structural elements.
- **LLM-Summary**: Standard approach using an LLM to rewrite/summarize the accumulated trajectory.
- **Hybrid approach**: Combines masking and summarization strategically.
- **Evaluation scaffold**: SWE-agent on SWE-bench Verified across five diverse model configurations; initial generalization evidence on OpenHands.

## 3. Key Evidence
- Environment observation tokens constitute ~84% of an average SWE-agent turn.
- Observation Masking matches LLM-Summary solve rate at approximately half the cost.
- Hybrid approach reduces costs by 7% compared to Observation Masking and 11% compared to LLM-Summary.
- Findings hold across five model configurations including Qwen3-32B, Qwen3-32B (thinking), and Qwen3-Coder 480B.

## 4. Limitations & Gaps
- Primary evaluation is on software engineering agents (SWE-bench); generalization to other domains is only initial evidence (OpenHands).
- SE domain has uniquely verbose and noisy observations; masking may be less effective in domains with sparser but higher-signal observations.
- Does not address cross-session memory preservation—only within-trajectory context management.
- The "lost in the middle" problem may affect masking differently in non-SE tasks where older observations contain less redundant information.

## 5. Relevance to Our Review
- **Context compression**: A critical counterpoint to the assumption that LLM summarization is always worth its cost.
- **Benchmarks**: Rigorous cost-effectiveness analysis on a standard software-engineering benchmark.

## 7. Personal Annotation
- The title's provocation is well-supported: simple masking is indeed competitive with LLM summarization in SE tasks.
- Important caveat for our review: the dominance of observation tokens (84%) is SE-specific; other agent types may have different token distributions.
- The hybrid result suggests there is still room for nuanced strategies, but the trend toward ever-more-complex summarization pipelines should be questioned.
